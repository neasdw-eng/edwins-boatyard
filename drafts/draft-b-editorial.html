The Cover Story

The art of keeping a boat
on the water.

For four decades, Edwin's Boat Yard has been the quiet authority on the western shore of Man-O-War Cay — a place where the marine railway still creaks the way it did in 1976, where the gelcoat work is done by men who learned it from their fathers, and where the customers come back, year after year, because some craft is too good to chase.

We haul. We store. We patch what the reef takes and we send you out again — with a hull as fair as the day she was launched.

What the yard can do.

i.

Haul-Out & Launch

Marine railway service for power and sail. Careful, deliberate, and as old-fashioned as it sounds.

ii.

Hull & Bottom

Pressure wash, sanding, barrier coat, anti-fouling — the full preparation, done by hand.

iii.

Long-Term Storage

Dry storage with hurricane preparation. Monitoring through the season; reports after every storm.

iv.

Emergency Repairs

Short-notice work, executed in a timely and efficient manner. We've never been known to drag our feet.

v.

Fiberglass & Gelcoat

Structural fiberglass repair and gelcoat restoration to a finish you'd be proud to invoice.

vi.

Mechanical

Engine, prop, shaft, and through-hull work. The unromantic things that determine whether a boat actually sails.

The Abacos made boats before they made anything else, and the men who built them taught the men who run this yard. We are not in a hurry. We are also never late.
